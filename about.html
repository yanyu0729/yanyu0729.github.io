About

My path into environmental science began with a personal awareness of how economic development, livelihoods, and environmental quality are deeply connected.

Where My Journey Began

I grew up in Ma’anshan, a heavily industrialized city in eastern China along the Yangtze River. The city’s development has long been shaped by steel production and mineral resources, which supported local livelihoods while also raising important questions about environmental quality, public health, and sustainable development.

Growing up in this context helped me understand that environmental challenges are rarely separate from people’s lives. Industry provided jobs and stability for many families, including my own, while also making visible the environmental costs of development. This early experience shaped my interest in how societies can balance economic opportunity with environmental protection.

My academic journey later took me from China to the United States through a 2+2 international undergraduate program. Studying across two educational systems broadened my perspective on environmental science and ultimately inspired me to pursue research at the intersection of agriculture, sustainability, and environmental decision-making.

B.S.

Anhui Agricultural University & Colorado State University

I completed a 2+2 international dual-degree undergraduate program between Anhui Agricultural University and Colorado State University, earning Bachelor of Science degrees from both institutions. Studying across two educational systems broadened my perspective on environmental science and strengthened my interest in sustainable agriculture, ecosystem modeling, and interdisciplinary approaches to solving environmental challenges.

M.S.

Emory University

At Emory University, I studied Environmental Sciences and investigated greenhouse gas emissions under different cover crop systems, further developing my interests in agricultural sustainability and climate-smart management.

Ph.D.

University of Maryland Center for Environmental Science

My doctoral research focused on nitrogen management, nutrient circularity, and sustainable agri-food systems. Through systems modeling, geospatial analysis, nutrient budgeting, and stakeholder engagement, I developed science-based strategies to improve agricultural sustainability in both the United States and China.

Postdoctoral Fellow

Georgetown University Earth Commons

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at Georgetown University Earth Commons, where I investigate the environmental and climate impacts of food systems and develop science-based strategies for more sustainable and resilient agriculture.
