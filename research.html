N Flow Analysis

I use nitrogen flow analysis to quantify how nitrogen moves through agricultural and food systems, including inputs from fertilizer, biological nitrogen fixation, feed, manure, crop harvest, animal products, environmental losses, and system accumulation.

Key themes

  • National and regional nitrogen budgets
  • Nitrogen use efficiency across crop and livestock systems
  • Environmental nitrogen losses and accumulation
  • Food-system nitrogen circularity
×

Statistical Drivers

I apply statistical modeling, geospatial analysis, and machine learning approaches to understand the factors shaping nutrient use efficiency and environmental outcomes. This work helps identify where interventions may be most effective.

Key themes

  • Spatial patterns of nitrogen use efficiency
  • Drivers of nutrient management outcomes
  • Regional heterogeneity in agricultural systems
  • Data-driven identification of management opportunities
×

Nutrient Recycling

My work on nutrient recycling focuses on improving manure nitrogen recovery, redistribution, and reuse. By better connecting crop and livestock systems, nutrient recycling can reduce reliance on external nitrogen inputs and help close nutrient loops.

Key themes

  • Manure nitrogen balance and recycling potential
  • Crop-livestock recoupling
  • Internal nutrient recycling strategies
  • Scenario analysis for sustainable food systems
×

Stakeholder Engagement

I engage researchers, policymakers, producers, and other stakeholders to connect scientific analysis with practical decision-making. This work supports more actionable, inclusive, and context-specific approaches to sustainable nitrogen governance.

Key themes

  • Stakeholder workshops and collaborative research
  • Science-policy-practice connections
  • Cross-country learning on nitrogen management
  • Pathways for sustainable food-system transformation
